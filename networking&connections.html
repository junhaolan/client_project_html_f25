Your Gateway to Building Professional Relationships

Developing your network is a key component of career success. The UMSI Networking & Connections Resource Hub is designed to empower you with the tools, strategies, and guidance you need to initiate and grow meaningful professional connections — whether you’re hoping to connect with peers, UMSI alumni, faculty, industry professionals, or potential mentors.

Why Networking Matters

  • Expand Your Opportunities: Many jobs and internships are found through connections, not just online postings.
  • Build Lasting Relationships: Connect with mentors, peers, alumni, and professionals who can support your journey.
  • Learn from Others: Gather insights, advice, and real-world knowledge to inform your academic and career decisions.
  • Gain Visibility: Raise your professional profile within UMSI and the broader information field.
  • Access UMSI Events & Platforms: Explore exclusive events and platforms, like UMSI Career Link, to connect with the UMSI community and employers.

Enhance Your Networking Skills

Building a strong professional network takes intention, practice, and the right strategies. At UMSI, we encourage students to develop essential relationship-building skills that can open doors throughout your academic and professional journey. Here are some practical ways to enhance your networking abilities:

  • Prepare Your Introduction: Craft a brief and engaging self-introduction that highlights your interests, background, and goals. This “elevator pitch” will help you make a positive first impression when meeting new people at events or online forums.
  • Start Meaningful Conversations: Don’t be afraid to ask questions and show genuine curiosity about others’ experiences. Listen actively and look for shared interests to establish common ground. Remember, networking is about building relationships, not just exchanging information.
  • Follow Up Thoughtfully: After meeting someone, follow up with a friendly message. Express your appreciation for the conversation, mention something you found valuable, and suggest ways to stay in touch. Consistent, polite follow-up can turn a brief interaction into a lasting connection.
  • Stay Involved in the UMSI Community: Attend networking events, join student organizations, and participate in group projects. These activities provide natural opportunities to meet classmates, faculty, and alumni who share your interests and can support your professional growth.
  • Leverage Online Platforms: Use digital tools such as campus forums or group chats to stay connected with peers and learn about new networking opportunities. Professional profiles and active engagement in online discussions can help you expand your reach beyond campus.

Developing your networking skills is a lifelong process. Each conversation and event is a chance to learn, connect, and grow. If you’re unsure where to start, reach out to the UMSI Career Development Office for personalized advice.
